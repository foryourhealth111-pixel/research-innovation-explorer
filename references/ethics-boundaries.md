# Ethics and Boundaries

## Default Stance

Treat this workflow as a way to find efficient, grounded incremental research ideas. Do not demean incremental work, and do not inflate it.

## Allowed

- stand on strong prior papers
- reuse open-source systems responsibly
- build a sharper abstraction around a real mechanism
- present narrow but defensible gains
- say clearly when the contribution is primarily engineering integration

## Not Allowed

- fabricate citations, proofs, experiments, or reviewer-facing claims
- hide that a method is mostly a combination of existing parts
- claim universal superiority from one benchmark pocket
- cherry-pick a middle `alpha` value and suppress the rest of the sweep
- write "A and B are special cases" without a real derivation or algorithmic mapping

## Preferred Attitude

Use language that respects the practical value of this work:

- low trial cost
- strong grounding in prior art
- good training for literature review, implementation, abstraction, and validation

That tone is legitimate. What is not legitimate is overstating novelty or certainty.

## If the Evidence Is Weak

- narrow the claim
- report the boundary
- reposition the contribution
- or drop the idea

Do not use wording to compensate for missing evidence.
